01

Assignment 1 — Ray Tracing

🔺
Two-Level BVH Acceleration
Triangles and spheres are placed in separate GeometryGroup BVHs, then combined under a root Group BVH. This two-level hierarchy minimises ray–primitive tests and handles mixed geometry efficiently. The dragon scene with tens of thousands of triangles renders correctly thanks to this structure.
🥚
Ellipsoid Intersection
Each sphere stores its inverse model matrix. At runtime the world-space ray is transformed into object space (w=1 for origin, w=0 for direction), intersected with a unit sphere, and the normal is brought back via (A⁻¹)ᵀ — the inverse-transpose rule. Enables the pinwheel ellipses seen in the table scenes.
💡
Blinn-Phong Shading
Full ka + kd + ks + ke material model. Diffuse uses the Lambert dot-product. Specular uses the Blinn half-vector H = normalize(L + V) raised to shininess. Shadow rays gate both terms per light.
📉
Light Attenuation
Point lights fall off with distance using att = 1/(c₁ + c₂d + c₃d²). Constants are parsed per-scene from the attenuation command. Directional lights are unattenuated (infinitely far).
🪞
Recursive Reflections
Surfaces with non-zero ks spawn mirror reflection rays using r = d − 2(d·n)n. Depth is tracked in the payload struct and capped at 5 bounces to prevent infinite recursion between reflective surfaces.
🌑
Shadow Rays
Each lit surface fires a shadow ray per light before accumulating diffuse and specular. Point-light shadow rays use tmax = lightDist − ε so geometry beyond the light does not cast shadows. An epsilon offset avoids self-intersection.

  • maxdepth

Implementation Notes

Normal transformation uses the inverse-transpose matrix (n′ = (A⁻¹)ᵀ n) to ensure normals remain perpendicular to surfaces under non-uniform scale. Bounding boxes for transformed spheres are computed by transforming all 8 corners of the object-space AABB and taking world-space min/max — ensuring tight, correct bounds for the BVH. The OptiX trace depth is set to 12 with a shader-level cap of 5 to balance visual quality and performance.
